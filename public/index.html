Go A&E for medical emergencies

  • Sudden or severe pain, including chest pain
  • Breathing difficulty or shortness of breath
  • Uncontrolled bleeding of any kind
  • Sudden confusion or disorientation
  • Sudden dizziness, numbness, weakness or vision change
  • Coughing or vomiting of blood
  • Continuous vomiting or diarrhoea

Go GPFirst for non-emergency cases

The GPFirst programme encourages individuals with mild to moderate medical conditions to consult a GP before heading to A&E. This ensures timely medical treatment for their conditions and allows A&E to prioritize more critical cases.
